## Event Overview

The Singapore ACM SIGKDD Symposium 2026 is an in-person event organized by the Singapore ACM SIGKDD Chapter, held in parallel with AAAI 2026.

**Event Details:**
- **Date:** January 20, 2026
- **Venue:** Singapore University of Technology and Design (~12 min walk from AAAI 2026 venue at Singapore EXPO)
- **Format:** One-day symposium with keynotes, oral presentations, and poster presentations
- **Awards:** Best Presentation Awards and Best Poster Awards with certificates and cash prizes
- **Registration:** Opens soon
  - Student: $15 (before Jan 5, 2026)
  - Early bird: $25 (before Jan 5, 2026)
  - Regular/On-site: $30 (after Jan 5, 2026)

**Key Organizers:**
- **Honorary Chair:** Hady W. Lauw
- **General Chairs:** Yuan Fang, Roy Ka-Wei Lee
- **Program Chairs:** Guansong Pang, Zhu Sun

## Oral Presentations

### 1. Human-Pedagogy Inspired LLM Fine-Tuning Paradigm for Lifelong Learning and Continual Adaptation

**Publication Venue:** AAAI 2026
**Authors:** Nitin Vetcha
**Affiliations:** NUS (Visiting Scholar), IISc (UG B.Tech MnC)

**Description:** Presents a novel LLM fine-tuning approach inspired by human pedagogy principles to enable lifelong learning and continual adaptation in language models. Addresses catastrophic forgetting and enables models to learn new tasks without degrading performance on previously learned tasks.

---

### 2. Temporal Restoration and Spatial Rewiring for Source-Free Multivariate Time Series Domain Adaptation

**Publication Venue:** KDD
**Authors:** Peiliang Gong, Yucheng Wang, Min Wu, Zhenghua Chen, Xiaoli Li, Daoqiang Zhang
**Affiliations:** Nanyang Technological University

**Description:** Proposes a source-free domain adaptation method for multivariate time series. Uses temporal restoration to recover missing information and spatial rewiring to adapt feature relationships across domains without access to source data.

---

### 3. Polarized Communities meet Densest Subgraph: Efficient and Effective Polarization Detection in Signed Networks

**Publication Venue:** ACM Transactions on Knowledge Discovery from Data (TKDD)
**Authors:** Francesco Gullo, Domenico Mandaglio, Andrea Tagarelli
**Affiliations:** University of Calabria

**Description:** Develops efficient algorithms for detecting polarized communities in signed networks by combining densest subgraph techniques with network analysis. Relevant for understanding online communities, social media polarization, and conflict detection.

---

### 4. FedSC: Federated Learning with Semantic-Aware Collaboration

**Publication Venue:** KDD 2025
**Authors:** Huan Wang, Haoran Li, Huaming Chen, Jun Yan, Jiahua Shi, Jun Shen
**Affiliations:** University of Wollongong

**Description:** Introduces semantic-aware collaboration in federated learning to improve model performance across distributed, heterogeneous data sources. Enables federated systems to leverage semantic relationships between local models without centralizing data.

---

### 5. Discovering and Reasoning of Causality in the Hidden World with Large Language Models

**Publication Venue:** NeurIPS 2024 (extension under submission to JMLR)
**Authors:** Chenxi Liu, Yongqiang Chen, Tongliang Liu, Mingming Gong, James Cheng, Bo Han, Kun Zhang
**Affiliations:** MBZUAI & CMU

**Description:** Explores how LLMs can discover and reason about causal relationships in unobserved or hidden environments. Combines causal inference with LLM reasoning capabilities for discovering latent causal structures.

---

### 6. A Partition Cover Approach to Tokenization

**Publication Venue:** NeurIPS
**Authors:** Jia Peng Lim, Shawn Tan, Davin Choo, Hady W. Lauw
**Affiliations:** Singapore Management University

**Description:** Proposes a novel tokenization methodology based on partition cover theory. Offers improved efficiency and performance compared to standard tokenization approaches used in NLP.

---

### 7. How Do Images Align and Complement LiDAR? Towards a Harmonized Multi-modal 3D Panoptic Segmentation

**Publication Venue:** ICML
**Authors:** Yining Pan, Qiongjie Cui, Xulei Yang, Na Zhao
**Affiliations:** Singapore University of Technology and Design

**Description:** Investigates multi-modal fusion of image and LiDAR data for 3D scene understanding. Develops methods to harmonize different sensor modalities for improved panoptic segmentation.

---

### 8. Toxicity Red-Teaming: Benchmarking LLM Safety in Singapore's Low-Resource Languages

**Publication Venue:** EMNLP
**Authors:** Yujia Hu, Ming Shan Hee, Preslav Nakov, Roy Ka-Wei Lee
**Affiliations:** Singapore University of Technology and Design

**Description:** Conducts systematic safety testing (red-teaming) of LLMs in low-resource languages spoken in Singapore. Creates benchmarks for evaluating LLM toxicity and safety across linguistic contexts.

---

### 9. One Node One Model: Featuring the Missing-Half for Graph Clustering

**Publication Venue:** AAAI
**Authors:** Xuanting Xie, Bingheng Li, Erlin Pan, Zhaochen Guo, Zhao Kang, Wenyu Chen
**Affiliations:** University of Electronic Science and Technology of China & Singapore Management University

**Description:** Proposes a novel graph clustering approach where each node learns its own model, enabling more expressive and flexible clustering. Addresses the challenge of capturing node-specific patterns in graph data.

---

### 10. Not All Factors Crowd Equally: A Game-Theoretic Model of Alpha Decay ⭐

**Publication Venue:** Work-in-Progress
**Author:** Chorok Lee
**Affiliations:** KAIST

**Description:** Develops a game-theoretic model to explain heterogeneous alpha decay across investment factors. Shows that different factor types (mechanical vs. judgment-based) experience different crowding dynamics following a hyperbolic decay function α(t) = K/(1+λt). Proposes implications for factor selection and portfolio management.
